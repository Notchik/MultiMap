Tulovo (Belorussian Tula) is an agro-town (until 2008 - a village) in the Vitebsk region of the Vitebsk region of Belarus, 7 km east of Vitebsk. The population of the agro-town is 1792 people. The administrative and administrative center of the Tula Village Council.

The Vitba river flows along the northern part of the agro-town, the Pitomka river flows along the east, and the Tulovo reservoir is located to the north-east in a kilometer.

In Tula, the Vitebsk Agricultural Experimental Station, established today in 1956, is now the Republican Unitary Agricultural Enterprise "Tulovo Experimental Base" [1]. Near the eastern outskirts of Tulovo there is a route for carrying out competitions on moto and autocross.
