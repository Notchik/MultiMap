The Leninsky district was founded in 1951, named after VI Lenin, in modern borders since 1977.

Before the revolution there was a working suburb of Lyakhovka on the territory of the district. In 1917, the Second Congress of the Western Front armies was held in the building of the Minsk City Theater (now the Yanka Kupala National Academic Theater), in February 1919 - the First All-Belarusian Congress of Soviets. In the building along Podgornaya Street (now Karl Marx Street) in March 1917 the people's militia of Minsk, headed by MV Frunze, was organized.
